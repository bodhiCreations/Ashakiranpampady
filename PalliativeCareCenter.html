Palliative Care Center

It is notable that, in Kerala, old aged people are higher in number in comparison to the younger generation. The responsibility to look after these aged people is with their own children. However, due to changing priorities, this is often overlooked. Here arises the significance of the Palliative Care Centre and its mission to relieve suffering and to improve the quality of life for people of all ages with serious, chronic and life threatening illnesses.

Within a year of closing down Asha Kiran, Asha Kiran Palliative Care Center was born in the same building in Pothenpuram, Pampady. It was inaugurated by Mr. Oomen Chandy, the honorable Chief Minister of Kerala on March 02, 2014.

Presently 17 patients, who are partly disabled and bed ridden due to stroke, cancer, paralyzed by spinal cord injuries, old age complaints and other ailments, are admitted here. Most of the patients are unable to care for themselves and require full time support for their day to day activities.

Four trained nurses look after these patients day and night with a nominal salary. Notable is the contribution of a nurse, returned from the Gulf, who is providing her experience as a volunteer service. In addition, the inmates of Asha Kiran are benefitting from the full time service of Dr. Renjie George, retired Chief Medical Officer from Homoeo Hospital, Kurichy, Kottayam, free of charge. Moreover, Mr. Babu Zacharia, a retired teacher, is dedicating his valuable time as a full time coordinator.

The daily diet food, various types of medicines, nurse’s care, and hygiene are offered with no cost to patients. Approximate total monthly expense for these services amounts to ninety thousand to one hundred thousand Indian Rupees. This institution is presently running with the assistance of our various kind hearted brothers and sisters.
